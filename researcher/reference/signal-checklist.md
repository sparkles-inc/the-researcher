# Signal Checklist

Every signal the researcher evaluates, organized by category, with specific thresholds from the founder's experience.

## Reviews & Reputation

| Signal | What to look for | Threshold / Opinion |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Review count | Total Google reviews | Under 16 = below trust threshold. Clients won't trust you yet. |
| Rating | Google star rating | 4.7 minimum to be credible. Strive for 4.9. Below 4.7 = something is wrong. |
| Low count + low rating | Under 20 reviews with bad score | Growing pain. One bad review tanks it. Fixable with volume. Consider starting fresh. |
| High count + low rating | Over 20 reviews with bad score | Pattern. Service quality issue. Needs coaching before marketing. |
| Review quality | Detailed vs generic | "Great job thanks" = asked friends. Detailed stories = real satisfied clients. |
| Review responses | How business responds | No response = don't care. Copy-paste = motions. Thoughtful = coachable. |
| Negative review patterns | What complaints repeat | Same complaint 3+ times = real operational problem. Random complaints = noise. |
| Review recency | When last reviews were posted | Declining review velocity = possible churn or declining quality. |

## Website & Digital Presence

| Signal | What to look for | Threshold / Opinion |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Website exists | Do they have one at all | No website in 2026 = biggest visibility gap possible. |
| Mobile responsive | Does it work on a phone | Most clients search on mobile. Not mobile-friendly = doesn't work. |
| Load time | How fast it loads | Over 3 seconds = losing visitors. |
| Photos | Stock vs real team photos | Stock rubber gloves = template, didn't try. Real team = they care. |
| Owner/team info | About page content | No face behind the business = low trust. People hire people. |
| Clutter level | How busy the page is | Cleaning business websites are ironically often cluttered. Clean design = professional. |
| Copy quality | How they describe themselves | Too wordy, too noisy = bad. Clear and emotional = good. |

## Services & Specialization

| Signal | What to look for | Threshold / Opinion |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Service types | How many they offer | Residential + commercial + carpet + windows + etc = spread thin. |
| Niche focus | Specialized or generalist | Niche = signals quality. "We do everything" = likely not great at any of it. |
| Package structure | Standard vs deep vs move-in/out | Differentiated packages = they understand the business. "Cleaning" = commoditized. |
| Creative offers | Gift cards, bundles, seasonal | Innovation here signals a marketing-minded owner. Rare and valuable. |
| Inclusions listed | What's included per service | Clear inclusions = professional. "Call for details" = hiding something or disorganized. |

## Pricing

| Signal | What to look for | Threshold / Opinion |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Hourly equivalent | Calculate from their prices | Under $60/hr = undercharging. Probably burning out or underpaying cleaners. |
| Flat rate | Same price regardless | "$99 any home" or "$75 cleaning" = broken pricing model. Not accounting for variables. |
| Frequency pricing | Weekly vs bi-weekly vs monthly | No frequency discount = leaving money on the table. |
| Quote form | Online quoting available | No way to get a price without calling = friction. Losing after-hours leads. |
| Recurring pricing | 1-2 bed: $120-225 / 3-4 bed: $180-400 | Below these ranges = undercharging. Significantly above = luxury (verify the brand supports it). |

## Booking & Lead Flow

| Signal | What to look for | Threshold / Opinion |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Online booking | Can you book without calling | No online booking = losing clients every day, especially after hours. |
| Intake form | Structured way to request a quote | Even a simple form shows they have a process. |
| Phone answered | Do they pick up | Missed call with no follow-up = lost lead. |
| Response time | How fast they respond | First responder gets the job 78% of the time. Under 5 minutes = good. Under 1 minute = excellent. Over 1 hour = losing leads. |
| Follow-up system | What happens if you don't book | Most cleaning businesses don't follow up. The lead just dies. |
| Contact channels | Phone, email, text, chat, social DMs | More channels = more accessible. Phone only = limited. |

## Trust & Credibility

| Signal | What to look for | Threshold / Opinion |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Background checks | Do they mention it | Huge differentiator. Not everyone does it. Should be advertised. |
| Insurance | Mentioned on site | If it's not on the website, it might as well not exist to clients. |
| Guarantee | Satisfaction, re-clean, or nothing | A guarantee reduces client risk and increases conversion. |
| Named cleaners | "Maria will clean" vs "we'll send someone" | Naming builds trust. Anonymous does not. |
| Awards/partnerships | Nextdoor Fave, BBB, Cleaning for a Reason | Trust signals that cost nothing and matter a lot. |
| Referral program | Do they incentivize referrals | Shows they think about growth systematically. |
| Eco-friendly | Natural products, green positioning | Growing differentiator. Clients with kids and pets care. |
| Cancellation policy | Visible and clear | Professional businesses have this visible. |

## Red Flags for Struggling Businesses

When diagnosing a business, probe for these. Each one is a symptom of deeper problems:

| Signal | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| No written agreements with clients | Covert contracts destroying relationships. Unspoken expectations = guaranteed complaints. |
| Flat-rate pricing without variable assessment | Losing money on complex homes, overcharging simple ones. No walkthrough process. |
| Giving prices over the phone | Low close rates. The walkthrough is where the sale happens. |
| No distinction between maintenance/deep/special | Scope creep, client confusion, unprofitable jobs. |
| Hiring on gut feeling | No scorecard, no evaluation system. Will hire wrong and pay for it. |
| No cancellation cost formula | Undercharging for no-shows or not enforcing cancellations at all. |
| Clients scattered across the metro | Commute eating all profit. No geographic hub strategy. |
| No complaint-handling system | Repeating the same mistakes, losing clients to preventable problems. |
| No seasonal upsell strategy | Revenue dips blamed on "slow months" that aren't actually slow. |
| Taking every client who calls | No scorecard. Draining time on bad fits who will leave bad reviews. |
| No satisfaction guarantee | Losing sales to competitors who offer one. |
| Texting prospects instead of calling | Missing 67% of walkthrough conversions. |
| No credentials visible | ID badges, certifications, third-party verification missing from walkthroughs. |
| Not tracking cost of problems | Can't make data-driven decisions about where money leaks. |

## Operations & AI Readiness

| Signal | What to look for | Threshold / Opinion |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Software visible | Jobber, Launch27, Housecall Pro | Booking widget or client portal link = they use technology. |
| Quote form/calculator | Online pricing tool | If they have one, they understand process. If not, everything is manual. |
| Indeed postings | Are they hiring | Hiring 1 = replacing. Hiring 4 = growing. Constant postings = turnover. |
| Social media | Posting frequency | Once a week = healthy. Nothing in 6 months = too busy or gave up. Not about being an influencer. Just alive. |
| Google Ads | Are they running paid ads | Important channel for local service businesses. Running ads = investing in growth. |
| Before/after photos | Showing their work | Confidence in quality. Good for trust and conversion. |
