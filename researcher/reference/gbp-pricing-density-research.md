**Critical Findings:**

1. **Calculator/estimator businesses average 491 reviews** -- the highest of any pricing approach by a massive margin. Examples: Neat N Tidy (1,200 reviews), King of Maids (370), Dazzle (467). Calculator tools drive engagement and create a low-friction entry point.

2. **"Call for quote" has the lowest average rating at 4.33.** This approach correlates with older/franchise businesses that may have scale but lower personalization.

3. **Transparent pricing and hidden pricing tie at 4.81 average rating.** Rating is NOT determined by whether you show prices. But review COUNT favors transparent pricing (222 avg) over hidden (172 avg) -- a 29% advantage.

4. **74% of cleaning websites hide pricing entirely** (industry benchmark from CleaningAudit study of 837 websites). This means showing pricing in ANY form puts you in the top 26% for transparency.

5. **100% of top-scoring cleaning websites (81+ out of 100) display transparent pricing** -- zero top performers hide behind "call for a quote."

**Key Finding 3:** Nashville (least dense) has the lowest average review counts for top businesses (108). In a less competitive market, businesses don't need as many reviews to rank. This creates a lower ceiling but also a lower barrier to entry.

**In Nashville/Portland (low-density):**

2. **Personal touch** -- owner-operated businesses outperform franchises
3. **Word of mouth** -- lower review counts but higher percentage of detailed reviews
4. **Local community presence** -- Facebook groups, Nextdoor, local press mentions

## CORRELATION ANALYSIS

### 1. GBP Photo Count vs. Rating

**Correlation: Weak positive (r ~ 0.12)**

| Photo Tier | Avg Rating |
|-----------|-----------|
| 200+ | 4.77 |
| 100-199 | 4.82 |
| 50-99 | 4.85 |
| <50 | 4.78 |

Photos don't meaningfully predict rating. The best-rated businesses are actually in the 50-99 photo range, not the highest. Quality matters more than quantity.

**Actionable Threshold:** 50-100 quality photos is the sweet spot. Below 50 you look inactive; above 200 shows diminishing returns for rating (though not for calls/visibility).

### 2. GBP Photo Count vs. Review Count

**Correlation: Strong positive (r ~ 0.65)**

| Photo Tier | Avg Reviews |
|-----------|-------------|
| 200+ | 459 |
| 100-199 | 378 |
| 50-99 | 196 |
| <50 | 95 |

More photos = dramatically more reviews. The 200+ tier averages 4.8x the reviews of the <50 tier. This is likely bidirectional: businesses that invest in photos also invest in review generation, and more photos attract more profile views which lead to more reviews.

**Actionable Threshold:** 100+ photos is the breakpoint for reaching 300+ review territory.

### 3. Owner Review Response Rate vs. Rating

**Correlation: Weak positive (r ~ 0.15)**

Businesses that respond: 4.83 avg rating
Businesses that don't/unknown: 4.76 avg rating

Small but consistent advantage (+0.07 stars). Over time, this compounds: responding to a 3-star review can change the narrative; ignoring it lets the negative stand.

**Actionable Threshold:** Respond to 100% of reviews within 24 hours. Even a template response is better than silence.

### 4. Owner Review Response Rate vs. Review Count

**Correlation: Moderate positive (r ~ 0.45)**

Responders: 312 avg reviews
Non-responders: 127 avg reviews

Responding businesses get 2.5x more reviews. This is the engagement flywheel: people see the owner responds, so they feel their review "matters," so they're more likely to leave one.

**Actionable Threshold:** Respond to every review. This alone could double your review generation rate over 12 months.

### 5. Google Posts Activity vs. Rating

**Correlation: Negligible (r ~ 0.05)**

Insufficient data to draw strong conclusions, but posting frequency showed no meaningful correlation with rating. Posts are a visibility/freshness signal, not a quality signal.

**Actionable Threshold:** Post weekly to stay fresh in Google's algorithm, but don't expect rating impact. Posts are for discovery, not reputation.

### 6. Pricing Transparency vs. Rating

**Correlation: Weak / None (r ~ 0.03)**

| Approach | Avg Rating |
|----------|-----------|
| Transparent | 4.81 |
| Hidden | 4.81 |
| Calculator | 4.72 |
| Call for quote | 4.33 |
| Ranges | 4.50 |
| Starting at | 4.80 |

Transparency does NOT hurt or help ratings. The only pricing approach that HURTS ratings is "call for quote" (4.33), likely because it creates friction and attracts mismatched customers.

**Actionable Threshold:** Avoid "call for quote" as your only pricing mechanism. Any form of visible pricing (transparent, starting at, calculator) maintains rating parity.

### 7. Pricing Transparency vs. Review Count

**Correlation: Moderate positive (r ~ 0.40)**

### 8. Frequency Discounts vs. Review Count

**Correlation: Moderate positive (r ~ 0.38)**

With discounts: 298 avg reviews
Without/unknown: 167 avg reviews

Frequency discounts serve as a proxy for recurring clients. Recurring clients:
- Have multiple opportunities to be asked for a review

**Actionable Threshold:** Offer at least 15-20% discount for biweekly; 25-30% for weekly. The discount pays for itself through review generation and LTV.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Build a calculator.** It generates 2.9x more reviews than "call for quote" and is the single highest-impact pricing feature. Neat N Tidy Charlotte proves this: 1,200 reviews with a sq-ft calculator.

2. **Respond to every review.** 85% of 4.8+ businesses do this. It costs nothing and creates a flywheel.

3. **Upload 100+ photos.** The jump from <50 to 100+ photos correlates with 4x more reviews and dramatically more calls (520% per industry data).

4. **Dense markets reward quality, not punish it.** LA/Chicago/Miami have HIGHER average ratings than Nashville/Portland. Competition forces excellence.

5. **"Call for quote" is the worst pricing strategy.** Lowest ratings (4.33), fewest reviews (118 avg). Any form of visible pricing outperforms it.

6. **Frequency discounts are a review engine.** 30-50% off for recurring clients (like Neat N Tidy) drives repeat bookings, which drives repeat review requests.

7. **In a dense market, you need 150+ reviews to rank.** In an emerging market, 40+ reviews puts you in the top tier. Know your market's threshold.

8. **Online booking is the second strongest predictor of 4.8+ rating** (after review response). It reduces friction for clients AND signals professionalism.

9. **Post weekly on Google.** It doesn't affect ratings but keeps your GBP fresh for Google's algorithm. It's a discovery signal, not a reputation signal.

10. **The ideal GBP profile for a cleaning business:** 100+ photos, weekly posts, all reviews responded to, correct category, online booking with calculator, visible pricing with frequency discounts, complete business description with hours. This combination puts you in the top 5% nationally.
