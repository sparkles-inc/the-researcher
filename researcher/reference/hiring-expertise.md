# Hiring Expertise

## Philosophy
Hire for attitude, train for skill. A great attitude with teachable gaps beats impressive credentials with a bad personality every time.

## The hiring funnel (7 stages)
1. **Application screening** - Writing quality is the first filter, not experience. Can they communicate professionally?
2. **Text outreach** - Response speed, tone, professionalism are all signals. 3 days to reply = 3 days to reply to clients.
3. **Written screening questions** - 8 qualifying questions (own supplies? car? insurance? background check? jobs completed? availability?). Hard disqualifiers surface here.
4. **Phone interview** - The real filter. One killer question separates pros from amateurs.
5. **Education session** - Front-load standards and expectations BEFORE any contract.
6. **Background check** - Non-negotiable before trial.
7. **Trial clean** - Real performance at a trusted host home with detailed feedback rubric.

## The one question that matters most
> "Walk me through how you clean a home professionally. Start from: you're in your car about to go inside."

What you're listening for: some version of "top to bottom, left to right." That separates someone who cleans professionally from someone who cleans their own apartment. A professional has a system. An amateur wings it.

## Hard disqualifiers
- No car (vehicle required, no exceptions)
- Only commercial/janitorial/hotel experience, no residential (completely different skills)
- Theft on background check (forever no)
- "I just really need a job" energy with no cleaning track record
- No response to screening questions within 24 hours

## Red flags
- Vague residential claims ("some cleaning," "helped family") without specific paid clients
- Established career in another field with zero cleaning history (applying casually)
- Geographic scatter (work history jumps cities every year)
- Commercial experience masquerading as residential (carpet cleaning, window washing, hotel housekeeping don't transfer)

## Positive signals
- Professional summary mentions cleaning (it's their identity)
- "House cleaning" or "residential cleaning" in job title
- Can articulate their cleaning process with specificity
- Has own supplies, vehicle, insurance
- 50+ completed jobs, 10+ distinct clients
- Responds quickly, writes clearly, asks smart questions

## 90-day probation
- No call-outs, no complaints, no coverage swaps during probation
- Rated on: professionalism, punctuality, reliability, checklist compliance, client feedback
- Poor performance = no full offer. Don't drag it out hoping they improve.
- Cap new cleaners at 16 hrs/week during onboarding

## Trial clean
- Use a trusted host home (someone who knows what good looks like)
- Detailed feedback form with specific criteria, not vibes
- Provide specific feedback: what they did well, what needs work
- Host gets free cleaning in exchange. Protect this relationship.

## The meta-lesson
Every stage of the funnel exists to prevent a bad hire from reaching your clients. The cost of a bad hire is always higher than the cost of being picky. Reject faster, interview slower, never skip the trial clean.
