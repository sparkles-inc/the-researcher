# Client Management Expertise

## Complaint handling

### Core policy
Full price or full refund. No discounts. No splitting the difference. Most clients are good people and will pay full price. Very few actually take the refund.

### 24-hour re-clean guarantee
If something was checked off but not done properly, client notifies within 24 hours and you re-clean that specific area free. After 24 hours, the window closes. Non-negotiable.

### Always pick up the phone
Voice and presence resolve 90% of conflicts that text escalates. Never handle complaints over text or email if you can call.

### Resolution framework
1. Thank them for alerting you
2. Acknowledge the problem (never argue)
3. Agree it's unacceptable, you're there to help
4. Clarify your authority to fix it
5. Explain next steps and give a timeline
6. Ask if there's anything else
7. Fix it (re-clean, refund)
8. Confirm with client it's resolved
9. Ask for a review on how you handled it (complaints can become positive reviews)
10. Document and track the cost
11. Report to team, reward employees who solve problems well

## Lead qualification

### The goal of the first call
Book the walkthrough, NOT give a price. 67% more people agree to a walkthrough if they hear your voice vs. text. Always get them on the phone.

### Pre-qualifying questions
- Lead source and motivation (why you vs. competitors?)
- Urgency and service type (one-time or recurring?)
- Current cleaning situation (DIY? how long since last professional clean?)
- Decision maker check: "Are you the person hiring me, or gathering facts?"
- Home details: bedrooms, bathrooms, sq ft, pets
- Self-assessment scale 1-10 (calibrates expectations before you show up)

### Minimum service price
Establishes you as premium. A $225 minimum filters out clients who want $75 cleanings.

## Red flag clients

Five patterns to recognize and either set extra expectations or decline:

1. **The Hotel Client** - Staying in a hotel during cleaning, expects 100% completion in compressed window. Never onboard without explicit expectation-setting.
2. **The Buffet Client** - Hasn't had professional cleaning in years. Expects everything perfect in one visit. Needs full package sequence education or a graceful decline.
3. **Unrealistic timelines** - Guests arriving, event pressure.
4. **Resists booking the full recommended package sequence** - Wants to cherry-pick.
5. **Scope creep** - "While you're here" requests. Treats service like all-you-can-eat.

## The walkthrough

The walkthrough is where the sale happens. It creates the permanent, customized checklist for that client.
- Go room by room. Mark what's included, what's excluded.
- Assess home condition and recommend the right package sequence.
- The 1-10 self-assessment calibrates expectations before you arrive.
- Annual re-walkthroughs maintain quality and create natural upsell and rate increase moments.

## Onboarding sequence

1. Intake form completed BEFORE virtual walkthrough (no form = no call)
2. Virtual walkthrough: 15-30 min video call, room by room, customize checklist live
3. After acceptance: schedule initial packages, build the maintenance plan
4. Expectation-setting is non-negotiable for every new client
5. Automated feedback text after every job
6. Additional manual text after first service
7. Leave paper checklists after maintenance and deep cleans

## Client scorecard

Rate every potential client 1-10 on each:
1. Are they polite?
2. Are they reasonable?
3. Do they have authority to pay?
4. Are they respectful of you and your business?
5. Are they going to be easy to work for?
6. Did they ask good questions to clarify expectations?
7. Did they give clear answers to your questions?
8. Can you realistically deliver what's being promised?

Score below 50 out of 80? Walk away. Don't take the client. Trust the scorecard. The cost of a bad-fit client is always higher than the revenue they bring.

## Credentials close more sales

Third-party verification, ID badges, training certifications. This is the missing piece from most walkthroughs. Clients need to trust before they buy, and credentials are the fastest trust shortcut. A business without visible credentials is leaving conversions on the table.

## Covert contracts destroy businesses

Hidden expectations that nobody put in writing. The client assumes the oven gets cleaned. The cleaner assumes it doesn't. Neither said anything. Three months later, the client is furious and leaves a 1-star review about something that was never discussed.

The fix: the walkthrough checklist IS the agreement. Everything included is checked. Everything excluded is visible. "We guarantee 100% satisfaction on everything we clean. We do not guarantee that we will clean 100% of everything." Quality, not scope.

## Website must-haves for cleaning businesses

A cleaning business website needs these pages at minimum: Home (hero + services + testimonials + booking button), Services, Book Now, About, Contact. FAQ and Reviews pages are bonuses that build trust.

**Booking flow:** "Book Now" button in the header AND the hero. Intake form collects name, email, phone, address, home size, special requests. Automated confirmation email. 24-hour reminder. Post-service follow-up requesting a review.

**SEO basics:** Every service page targets "[service type] + [city]." Full address and phone in footer. Google Business Profile claimed and linked. Blog posts targeting long-tail keywords if they have the bandwidth.

A business with no website is the biggest visibility gap possible. But a beautiful website with no booking is all show, no conversion.

## The overarching philosophy
Prevention beats resolution. Proper qualification, expectation-setting, and the intake-to-walkthrough sequence eliminate most complaints before they happen.
