# Must-Have Checklist

What every residential cleaning business needs to be operating properly. Used by the researcher to score operational completeness.

## The Basics (must have to be a real business)

- [ ] Business license / LLC filed
- [ ] Insurance (general liability, minimum)
- [ ] A phone number that someone answers
- [ ] A way to give quotes (form, phone, or in-person)
- [ ] Basic pricing structure (not "call for a price" with no framework)

**Score: out of 5.** Under 3 = not ready for services. They need foundations first.

## Operations (should have to run well)

- [ ] Cleaning packages defined (standard, deep, move-in/out at minimum)
- [ ] Cleaning checklist for each service type
- [ ] Cleaner vetting process (background checks, interviews, trial cleans)
- [ ] Scheduling system (even Google Calendar counts)
- [ ] Client communication system (confirmations, reminders)
- [ ] A way to collect payment (Venmo counts, but a real system is better)
- [ ] Cancellation / rescheduling policy

**Score: out of 7.** Under 4 = operational gaps that cost them clients and money.

## Growth (need these to scale)

- [ ] Website with online booking
- [ ] Google Business Profile optimized (photos, posts, correct categories)
- [ ] Review generation system (asking after every clean)
- [ ] Follow-up system for leads that don't book
- [ ] Recurring client management (automated scheduling)
- [ ] Before/after photos (trust building and content)
- [ ] Referral program
- [ ] At least one marketing channel (Google Ads, SEO, or social)

**Score: out of 8.** Under 4 = they've hit a growth ceiling and don't know why.

## Premium (AI-ready signals)

- [ ] Automated text-back for missed calls
- [ ] Pipeline tracking (lead to client journey)
- [ ] Pricing calculator or online estimator
- [ ] AI assistant for booking and communication
- [ ] Analytics (true hourly rate, client lifetime value, acquisition cost)
- [ ] Automated follow-up sequences
- [ ] Client portal (self-service booking, history, invoices)

**Score: out of 7.** These are what you SELL, not what you expect them to have. A score of 0-1 here means maximum opportunity.

## Total Score: out of 27

| Score | Assessment |
|-------|-----------|
| 0-8 | **Just getting started.** Needs foundations. Don't sell advanced tools. Give them the roadmap. |
| 9-14 | **Growing but gapped.** Has the basics, missing key operational and growth pieces. High opportunity. |
| 15-20 | **Solid but manual.** Good business, doing things the hard way. Ready for efficiency tools. |
| 21-24 | **Strong operator.** Most things in place. Ready for AI and automation. |
| 25-27 | **Rare.** Already has most of this. Sell strategic consulting, not tools. |

## How to Use This

Don't ask the business owner to fill this out. Score it yourself from available data. Where you can't determine a signal, mark it "unknown" and note it in the uncertainty section.

The gaps between what they have and what they should have ARE your sales opportunities.
