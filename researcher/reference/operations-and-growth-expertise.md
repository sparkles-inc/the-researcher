# Operations & Growth Expertise

## Pricing psychology

### Never use flat rates
Price by the job based on walkthrough assessment. Going rate for maintenance cleans: $25-45/hr. Goal: train upward toward $50-70+/hr.

### Start low, raise with skill
New cleaners should price low to fill their schedule fast, then raise rates as quality and reputation improve. Arrogance about pricing before earning it costs money.

### The true hourly rate gut punch
Show cleaners their TRUE hourly rate including unpaid admin, drive time, quoting, and texting. A cleaner who thinks she makes $50/hr discovers she actually makes $28/hr. This realization drives behavior change.

### Common objections and how to handle them
- **"You charge too much"** - Reframe value, don't discount.
- **"I don't make that much per hour"** - The client isn't comparing apples to apples (supplies, insurance, expertise).
- **"Other guys are cheaper"** - Cheaper means different service, not the same service for less.
- **"Take off rooms"** - Adjust scope, not rate. If they want fewer rooms, reprice accordingly.
- **"My other cleaner charged less"** - Previous cleaner may have been undercharging, cutting corners, or uninsured.

## Compensation structures

### Payout model
- Base pay: 50% of job value
- Profit share bonus: +10% for zero callbacks = 60% total for perfect job
- Higher tiers mean more money despite lower percentages: 60% of $100 ($60) beats 70% of $65 ($45)

### New cleaner rules
90-day trial, one job/day max, 16 hrs/week cap until trust is established.

## Upsell strategies

### Upsells are introduced during the walkthrough, not pitched after
The walkthrough questions naturally surface opportunities:
- "Do you have a leather care program for those sofas?"
- "Are your small appliances on a cleaning schedule?"
- "Is your pantry on a rotation for expiration dates?"

### Key upsell categories
- **Kitchen:** inside/outside cabinets, large/small appliances, pantry
- **Bathrooms:** "Icky tub/shower" (common for new accounts, treated as Special Package)
- **Closets:** coat, bedroom, linen, broom/supply
- **"Save Me" packages:** air filter replacement, toilet flappers, smoke detectors, baking soda fresheners. Low-effort recurring revenue.
- **Holiday packages:** guest rooms, tree trimming/removal, holiday decorating/cleanup. Seasonal revenue spikes.

### The deep clean close
"This bathroom has three appliances, one vanity, and the floor that fall in the deep clean category. Have we talked about how deep cleaning works? I'd love to schedule the deep clean right away." Close it during the tour, not in a follow-up email.

## Culture & retention

### Events
Bowling every ~6 months, December party, mid-year party. Simple, consistent, relationship-building.

### Incentive programs
- Weekly meeting attendance rewards
- Monthly spin-the-wheel with major prize
- Quarterly excellence bonuses for top performers
- Bonuses after X flawless jobs
- $15/job photo bonus (drives content creation AND quality accountability)

### Performance management
Underperformers get deprioritized for scheduling (fewer hours = natural consequence). Work stops immediately on termination decision.

## Territory strategy

Active adult communities are gold. One-story homes, newly retired, disposable income, consistent schedules. Work with realtors for co-marketing. Use NextDoor for referral marketing in specific neighborhoods.

"Oversaturation" is a concept, not a curse. There's always room if you carve a niche and narrow your territory. The businesses that struggle with competition are the ones trying to serve everyone everywhere.

## Seasonal upsell calendar

Revenue doesn't have to be flat. Month-by-month opportunities:
- **Jan-Feb:** Indoor deep cleaning, organizing (fill the slow season)
- **Mar-May:** Appliance cleaning, window washing, gutter cleaning
- **Jun-Aug:** Pressure washing, pantry cleaning, defrosting freezers, garage organizing
- **Sep-Oct:** Leaf removal, attic organizing
- **Nov-Dec:** Holiday decorating, guest rooms, tree trimming/takedown

Businesses without a seasonal strategy have revenue dips they blame on "slow months." Slow months aren't slow. They're just unplanned.

## The worksheet is a sales tool

The checklist itself isn't just accountability. It's a closing mechanism. A well-designed worksheet uses psychology, scarcity, and clear scope to close the sale during the walkthrough. "This is exactly what you're getting. This is what's not included. Here's what I recommend adding." The client sees professionalism, transparency, and expertise in one document.

## Growth strategy (Hormozi Value Ladder)

### The flywheel
Instagram teaches cleaning business skills, driving app downloads. The app collects data. Data identifies top cleaners. Top cleaners get invited into the network. Network cleaners become transformation stories. Stories fuel more content. Repeat.

### Four rungs
1. **Free** - Complete business OS (calculator, checklists, CRM, schedule). The Hormozi "give."
2. **Paid ($25/mo)** - Analytics, branded quotes, expense tracking. Signal detector for serious cleaners.
3. **Network (invite-only)** - Backup coverage, zero admin, AI operations, 70% payout. Invite criteria: 4.8+ reviews, $50+/hr avg, 6+ months, high reliability.
4. **Elite (invite from Network)** - $100+/hr, 60% payout, premium clients.

### The pitch
"Keep 100% of your pay. The fee keeps premium clients coming to you." At network level: zero admin burden, backup coverage, AI handles scheduling/invoicing/communication.
